ROI Comparison 8

Case 1: Intervention for High-Risk Individuals
  • Assumption: An 8% reduction in distractibility leads to a 4% increase in productivity.
  • Industry Average: Each hour of increased productivity is worth $45.
  • Calculation: For a person working 8 hours per day, a 4% increase in productivity equates to 0.32 extra productive hours, or $14.4 per day.
  • Annual ROI: $14.4/day * 250 working days/year = $3,600 increase in productivity per individual per year.
Case 2: Preventative Measures for Low-Risk Groups
  • Assumption: Maintaining low-risk status, as indicated by spending 1-2 hours on social media daily and experiencing lower validation seeking scores and sleep issues frequency, prevents a 5% decline in productivity due to mental health issues.
  • Industry Average: A 5% productivity decline equals a loss of $20 per 8-hour workday.
  • Calculation: Preventing this decline saves $20/day.
  • Annual ROI: $20/day * 250 working days/year = $5,000 saved per individual per year.
